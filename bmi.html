What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple measurement that uses your height and weight to estimate your body fat level. It's used by doctors, nutritionists, and fitness professionals worldwide as a quick health screening tool.

How is BMI calculated? BMI = weight (kg) / height² (m). For example, if you weigh 70 kg and are 1.70 m tall, your BMI is 70 / (1.70 × 1.70) = 24.2, which falls in the normal weight range.

Is BMI accurate for everyone? BMI is a useful general guideline, but it has limitations. Athletes with high muscle mass may have a high BMI without excess body fat. Older adults may have a normal BMI but still carry too much fat. Always consult a doctor for a complete health assessment.

What should I do with my BMI result? If your BMI is outside the normal range, consider discussing it with a healthcare provider. Small lifestyle changes — like walking 30 minutes a day or swapping sugary drinks for water — can make a measurable difference over time.
